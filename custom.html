Measure Once, Wear For Years

How to take your measurements.

Seven measurements get you a jacket that fits like it grew on you. Grab a soft tape, wear a light tee, and follow along. Not sure? Pick a standard size below, or request a free consult in the form.

Sizing for:
Show measurements in:

Numbers match the guide on the right.

  1. 1 Chest

    Around the fullest part of your chest, under the arms, tape level and snug. Keep it horizontal across your back.

  2. 2 Shoulder width

    From the bony edge of one shoulder, straight across the upper back, to the edge of the other.

  3. 3 Sleeve length

    From the shoulder edge, over a slightly bent elbow, down to the wrist bone.

  4. 4 Jacket / back length

    From the base of your neck down the spine to where you want the jacket to end (around the belt line).

  5. 5 Waist

    Around your natural waistline where you'd want the jacket to close — stand relaxed, don't suck in.

  6. 6 Bicep

    Around the fullest part of your upper arm, arm relaxed at your side.

  7. 7 Neck

    Around the base of your neck where a collar sits, one finger of room under the tape.

Five tips for an accurate result

  • Wear a light t-shirt — not bare-skin, not a thick jumper.
  • Keep the tape snug but not tight; it shouldn't dent your skin.
  • Have someone help, especially with shoulders and back length.
  • Stand naturally — relaxed shoulders, arms down, normal breathing.
  • Measure twice. If the two differ, measure a third time and trust the middle.
  • Ordering for a growing kid? Add one inch to chest and length for growing room.

Buying Custom, Without The Worry

Your questions, answered straight.

This is the worry with every custom order, so we built the process around preventing it. We cut to the measurements you submit and sanity-check them against your height and build before any leather is cut — if something looks off, we contact you first, not after. And if you'd rather not measure alone, request a free fitting consult and we'll guide you through it before you order.

Always. We work only with full-grain and top-grain genuine leather — sheepskin, goatskin (cabretta), lambskin and cowhide depending on the style. No bonded leather, no PU, no “vegan leather.” Each finished jacket ships with a short materials note describing the exact hide, weight and finish used for yours.

Because nothing exists until you order it, each jacket takes 2–3 weeks to cut and hand-stitch, then 3–6 days of tracked worldwide shipping. You'll get updates at confirmation, cutting and dispatch, with a tracking number once it's on the way. Need it for a date? Tell us in the notes and we'll be honest about whether we can make it.

A made-to-measure jacket is cut for one body — yours — so it can't be resold like a stock item. We're a young workshop and we're still finalising our formal returns and alterations policy. Ask us before you order and we'll give you a straight answer, in writing, for your specific piece — no fine print, no surprises.

In Sialkot, Pakistan — one of the world's great leather-craft cities — by makers who cut and sew each jacket by hand to your measurements. We ship worldwide from there, with duties and taxes made clear before you confirm.
